The Basics

Overview

Witches' Den is a game analyzer for Juho Snellman's Online Terra Mystica implementation (terra.snellman.net). As I begun playing more, I was constantly looking for how to improve, and this site is my attempt to build the tools and perform the research to answer that question. Specficially, I was looking to answer questions like:

  • How many points am I spending to leech power?
  • How many points does fav11 really get me?
  • Am I using the pass bonus tiles well for points?

My Goals

When I started building the site, I didn't know that Juho provided some of this information already -- now I do (you can find it if you click on your score ingame). Regardless, I envision taking this much farther. Some of the questions I'd like to answer:

  • On average, how many points do people gain from the Engineer's Stronghold
  • How many towns do most people found with X faction
  • Based on this board setup, which faction should I select?
  • Based on this board setup, and faction X, how many points should I get? Did I meet the goal? Why or why not?
  • How built up should I be by the end of round 3?
  • When I'm halflings, how many digs should I shoot for with a Round 4 spade scoring tile
Overall, I'm focused on learning from other plays to improve my play. One of my overall goals is to compare my scores against my "peers" (e.g. players +/-100 rating) and against the "best" players (e.g. top 10%) to see where I'm underscoring.

Focus on Points

As an overall guideline, my analysis is focused on scoring points, not winning. While the two items are intrisically linked, I think focusing on improving my score is a better guideline than focusing on beating others -- both because it's easier to measure in isolation and because I think it should be more stable over time. Perhaps these are faulty assumptions, but I plan to keep them for now.

How it Works (in short)

The specific data I gather is the data found in Snellman's ingame log. Specifically, I'm parsing those logs to determine what scoring sources a player has (e.g. current round bonus, current pass tile bonus, favors) and then checking every action against these for scoring. I'm reconciling this with Snellman's calculation every action. If you see any points labeled as "unknown", these are times when I come to a differnet count than Snellman -- please tell me if you see these and I'll investigate why and fix it. If you care about the technical details, see the later sections.

Technical Details

The site is modeled on the MEAN stack (Mongo, Express, Angular, Node), hosted on Windows Azure, with a focus on PaaS (platform-as-a-service). A largeimpetus for this project (to me) was to tryout a complete PaaS environment -- I have nothing but great things to say so far. I also wanted to leverage DSLs (Domain Specific Languages), so I was happy to include parsing Snellman's log as part of my work (I'm specifically using PegJS).

The production version of the site is hosted at witchesden.echternacht.org, built off of my master branch on GitHub. There is also a development version hosted at dwitchesden.echternacht.org, which is built off of my develop branch. I try to keep the dev site stable, but I'm sure I'll break it from time to time.

A much deeper look into the technology can be found hosted on GitHub.
